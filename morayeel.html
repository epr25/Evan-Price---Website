Moray Eel

Scientific Name: Muraenidae

Introduction

A common sight divers may see in coral reefs is Eels hiding within the rocky terrain of the ocean.

One of these sights could be the Moray Eel, which is one of the most well known species of eels.

They often have a bad reputation due to their frightening appearance and "aggression", though the latter is usually the case of hand-feeding or unaware divers.

Quick Facts

  • Moray eels hunt at night and are opportunistic carnivores; feeding off of fish, octopuses, crustaceans, and squid
  • Moray eels contain a protective mucus all over their body - which in some species is toxic, this mucus allows them to swim across the rocks without harm, and acts as a glue when they need to create burrows
  • Also mentioned in the Grouper page; they have a symbiotic relationship with Groupers, where they cooperate with one another to hunt prey - the Eel preventing the potential food from escaping in the rocks, while the Grouper patrols overhead
