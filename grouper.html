Goliath Grouper

Scientific Name: Epinephelus itajara

Introduction

This is the Goliath Grouper! Which is the largest species of Grouper Worldwide.

Despite it's simple appearance, there have been reports of Goliath Groupers (and other groupers) cooperating with eels to hunt prey together.

They also do not actively see humans as prey; despite their large appearance.

Quick Facts

  • Goliath Groupers can grow up to 8 feet and weigh up to 800 pounds

  • They can be found in coastal and nearshore waters that are located around manmade structures or muddy bottoms
  • They are opportunistic predators that mostly feed on slower bottom moving species, such as crabs
  • They are currently listed as a critically endangered species, as a result of overfishing and habitat destruction from humans
